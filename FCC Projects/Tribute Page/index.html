The man who shot for the stars

Galileo di Vincenzo Bonaiuti de' Galilei

Galileo Galilei was a man who lead on a pretty fascinating life. Below is a short timeline of what he accomplished and went through in his lifetime. For further information please visit this Wikipedia Page.
  • 15 February 1564 Pisa, Duchy of Florence - Galileo is born
  • 1581 - Enrolls in the University of Pisa (Italy) to pursue becoming a physician
  • 1586 - Publishes a book on the hydrostatic balance that catches word amongst scholars
  • 1588 - Becomes an art teacher at Accademia delle Arti del Disegno
  • 1589 - Appointed to the chair of mathematics in Pisa
  • 1591 - Beloved father passes away and leaves behind Michelagnolo, Galileo's younger brother that he must care for
  • 1592 - Moved to the University of Padua where he taught geometry, mechanics, and astronomy until 1610
  • 1593 - Invents the thermometer, however there are not many proved sources
  • 1597 - Creates an improved version of a geometric compass for cannons and surveyors, ends up selling ~100
  • 1604 - Kepler's Supernova appears in the sky
  • 1608 - The first practical telesope patent was created by Hans Lippershey
  • 1609 - Galileo creates a telescope with around 3x magnification, it is unknown whether or not Galileo created the first telescope
  • 25 August 1609 - Demonstrates a telescope with 8x-9x magnification to lawmakers
  • 1610 - Creates the first ever published scientific work on observations made through a telescope (Sidereus Nuncius, which a copy of the original has sold for $662,500 in 2010)
  • 7 January 1610 - Discovers Jupiter's moons causing a revolution in astronomy
